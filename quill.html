                            Quill

Around the time when the ChatGPT 3.5 API was released, my friends and I recognized the potential and tried to hop on the bandwagon before it took off. One issue I thought could be solved with the new tech is the problem of being confused when reading something online. In our experiences, it is very common to be reading a news or tech article and be confused about a certain word, phrase, or paragraph. And the best solution at the time was to open a new tab in Google or ChatGPT and do the research yourself.

So, imagine all of this done for you, and all you have to do is highlight something you're confused about and let AI do the rest.

Quill is a Chrome extension that integrates ChatGPT into your browsing experience. Users can highlight text, hover over a dot, and get an AI-generated response explaining that text. Users can then ask to get examples, summarize, or ask follow up questions about the text.

A gif showing how Quill can be used.

We created Quill using Javascript, HTML, and CSS. Chrome extensions require a service worker to handle the back-end, and a manifest to describe the specs and requirements of the project. Besides those things, the rest was just a lot of Javascript logic, dealing with the ChatGPT API, and styling everything with HTML and CSS.

You can download Quill for yourself here. In around a month of advertising, we garnered over 300 users!
