          Allegro Speaker

The senior capstone project was the culmination of my high school's STEM program. Our task was to put together everything we have learned over the past four years and apply that knowledge to design and build a new product not currently on the market. We followed the engineering design process to formulate a clear idea, design and build prototypes, and finally, market and present our product to the public.

My group's idea was to design a speaker (our product name was "Allegro Speakers") that sounds good over a wide listening area. The problem we identified was that higher frequencies on current speakers are generally highly directional, leading to many speakers having a somewhat narrow listening area where the speaker sounds best. Our proposed solution was to implement waveguides for the high and low range drivers on a speaker that effectively disperses high frequencies. We designed an enclosure for this speaker, which included the waveguide for the tweeter and an additional waveguide for the woofer to maximize sound quality.

Unfortunately, the COVID pandemic forced us to take the project home and work remotely, starting in mid March. I was the most involved with the project, so I inherited it as a personal project. At this point we had only started our CAD model of the enclosure, leaving me plenty of work to do by myself. I designed most of the parts of the enclosure, before 3D printing them and assembling together with some electronic parts I had purchased online. I borrowed an amplifier and a USB microphone from a local sound engineer who was a huge help in this project.

Once the speaker was built and connected to an amplifier, I was able to connect it to my computer and test it out. I ran several frequency response tests using Room EQ Wizard and adjusted the EQ of the drivers through the amplifier using a platform called Armonia. Once the frequency response curve was relatively flat, I took screenshots of the data and began writing the report.

The report describes in detail our process for this project, as well as the data gathered, an analysis of that data, and our conclusions about the project. View the pdf of the finished report here.
