                     Hydr8Mate

IdeaHacks is a hardware hackathon held annually at UCLA, and is the largest hardware hackathon on the West coast. Me and a few friends decided to do it in our second year, when the theme was "The Great Outdoors". We had 48 hours to design and create a product, renting out equipment if needed. We decided to create a water bottle level checker and app that tells you how much water you've drinken throughout the day.

The only problem is that we started at around 8pm the night before it was due. This meant that we pulled an all nighter and didn't achieve quite as much as we were hoping. However, the product was still good enough to earn us a top 10 spot by the judges.

The final product was a water bottle with an ESP32 microcontroller and ultrasonic sensor inside. The sensor would collect data that would be processed by the microcontroller, which would then be sent to the application via Bluetooth. The application would then display that data (i.e. current volume of water in the bottle, the amount of water the user has had in the day, height and area of water bottle for calibration purposes, and the amount of time surpassed since last sip taken) for the user to see.
