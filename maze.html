Internet of Things: The Maze

ENGR 96C is a class at UCLA that utilizes the STMicroelectronics Sensortile device, equipped with several instruments including an accelerometer and gyroscope, to teach students about machine learning and internet of things technology. The majority of the quarter is spent completing tutorials that taught me how to use the device and write code to change how it operates.

For the final project, however, we were given more freedom on what we wanted to do. I chose to design a maze that the player can navigate through by designating their own motions. You can see a video demonstration of my project below.

In the video, I begin by training the device to recognize six distinct two-part motions. Then the device randomly assigns weights to input neurons and begins calculating outputs. These outputs are compared to the ground truth values from the beginning and the weights are adjusted accordingly. After a few seconds of training, the system is ready for the game to be played.

Once the player has decided which room they wish to enter, they execute the double tap command, and complete their two part motion to choose their room. The numbering of motions goes from left to right, so for example, in Room 1, if the player wished to enter Room 3 (which is displayed as the left-most room), they would execute the first motion they trained. The player can explore the maze for as long as they wish, assuming they never reach one of the game-ending rooms.
